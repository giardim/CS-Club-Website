Who We Are

The Computer Science Club is focused on software-centric projects and events. The club introduces students to the world of Computer Science in a collaborative way. We aim to familiarize our members with various computer systems, operating systems and programming languages. With our CS faculty support -- which hold lectures for anyone to attend -- we hope to include a variety of students from all majors.

What We Do

Every semester, the club votes on what project they want to pursue. Through these projects we learn how to code in a team environment. We want to make sure students are prepared for real world in a fun and relaxed setting.

This semester we had the opportunity to work on an arcade cabinet, which will allow students to create and publish their games for their peers to play.

We also had funding from the Computer Science department to purchase "Duck Cars", which which are cars that we can program to drive autonomously.

In the future, we hope to host Hackathons for students from SUNY schools and get people excited to program.

Where We Meet

The Computer Science club meets every Tuesday at 1:00pm in C112 in Kunsela Hall. We hope to see you all there!
